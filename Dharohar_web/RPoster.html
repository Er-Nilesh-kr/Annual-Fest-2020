Rules and Regulations:

Theme of Poster: Innovations for the benefit of Society

About IEEE:


The Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) is a professional association with its corporate office in New York City and its operations center in Piscataway, New Jersey. It was formed in 1963 from the amalgamation of the American Institute of Electrical Engineers and the Institute of Radio Engineers. Today, it is the world's largest association of technical professionals with more than 400,000 members in chapters around the world. Its objectives are the educational and technical advancement of electrical and electronic engineering, telecommunications, and computer engineering and allied disciplines.

About Organizer:


IEEE Student Branch was established at Quantum School of Technology, Roorkee in January 2017 for encouraging the students, research scholars and faculties for exploring the information in the field of Mechanical, Electrical and Electronics, and Computer Science for Research and Development purpose. After the approval of the branch in January 2017, we have also taken the membership of one affinity group (Women in Engineering, Approved in March 2017) and one technical chapter (Robotics and Automation Society, Approved in June 2017).

The student branch provides members the opportunity to interact with the researchers and professionals around the world through the various events like workshops, seminars, conferences etc.

With the inception of Quantum University in 2018, the Student Branch has been renamed to IEEE Student Branch at Quantum University and has showcased various events.

About Event:


IEEE student branch at Quantum University, Roorkee invites students and research scholars (Graduates and Post Graduates) from various fields of Engineering, Health Science, Agriculture, Management, Arts to showcase their skills acquired in their respective domain in form of different projects which benefits the society in one or the other way.

Poster Presentation competition instructions:-


•The poster must be depicted/drawn on white sheet of chart size. The poster must not include any printed material.
• The completed artwork must be on a single chart and in relevance to the theme of the event. The poster should also indicate how it benefits the society.
• The poster must not include any National flags/slogans.
• The poster must not represent any particular individual, organization, religion or brand name.
• Name and class/course of participant as well as college’s name and address must be clearly mentioned on the front of poster (bottom right corner in a rectangular space of 60*40 mm) in the English language.
• All the participants will have to report on March 19, 2020 by 2:00 pm in the university campus along with their posters. They must have their institute ID cards along with them.
• Participants are required to reach the venue on March 20, 2020 at least 30 minutes before the start of competition and display their posters in the given area.
• All posters submitted for competition shall become the property of organizing team.

Judgement Criterion:


•Originality of idea – 15


It must show the ability to be inventive and creative.

•Relevance to the theme – 10


The design should effectively communicate from the given theme.

•Aesthetic appeal – 15


The Poster design must captivate the judges’ senses, taking into consideration the aesthetic principles of symmetry, focal point, pattern, perspective, dimensionality and proportion.

•Question and answer round – 10


The decision of judges regarding winner and runner up in would be final and binding on all. The results would be announced on March 20, 2020.
